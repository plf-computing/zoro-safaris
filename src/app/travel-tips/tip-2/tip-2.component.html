WHAT TO PACK

If you are about to leave for Kenya these tips on what to bring will be essential!

Packing for your trip to Kenya doesn't have to be stressful. So follow our tips and you'll find everything you need to enjoy your trip with peace of mind.

These essentials are really few
Let's discover them together

Before starting to choose the most suitable clothes for the trip, you need to take into account the period of your stay . The hot season is from October to March, temperatures never fall below 22 degrees and can even reach 35°! The cooler season is from April to September with temperatures around 27-30° but which can also drop to 20° with rain.

We advise against leaving from the end of March to June, the rainy season, but if you want to see animal migrations and you travel during this period, it is necessary to bring clothing to protect yourself from the water. However, we refer you to our section on climate for more information.

However, Kenya is not just about the sea, so in our checklist we will also include items of clothing that can protect you in case of unforeseen events or, as in the case of Safaris, help you with the change in temperature based on the areas you are going to visit.

In any case, on the pages of each Tour or Excursion, you will find the recommended clothing or accessories to bring so you are not unprepared!

What else do you need to bring to Kenya?

Drugs

Usually you don't think of bringing medicines but preparing a small emergency quantity can be a great idea even if we hope you never have to use it! Let's see together what the medicines for our Travel Kit are , in brackets we give you some examples to help you understand what we are talking about :


Antipyretics and Painkillers (we recommend paracetamol as well as Tylenol)
Broad-spectrum antibiotics (such as amoxicillin)
Antidiarrheals (imodium or dissenten)
Lactic ferments (to help you restore the bacterial flora)
Disinfectant (such as amuchina, useful if you can't find water)
Mosquito repellent (e.g. autan tropical)
Antihistamines and cortisone (for erythema or allergic reactions)
Disinfectant wipes (in case of small wounds)
Eye drops (if you suffer from irritation from fine dust like in Safari)

Of course, don't forget the medicines you regularly take in Italy too!

In our experience you can avoid prophylaxis and vaccinations against malaria , in fact Kenya, with the exception of very internal areas, is not one of the areas most at risk. However, we always recommend consulting your personal doctor before leaving, who is certainly more up-to-date on your health status and can advise you on the best. However, every pharmacy in Kenya has a Malaria Kit available at a cost of €10.

We also want to reassure you by saying that in pharmacies in Kenya you can find the main drugs (with different names but the same active ingredient). If you need particular drugs, before leaving get a medical prescription so you can stay safe in case of need. Pharmacies are not present in the Safari areas, often there are uninhabited areas and therefore it could be difficult to find certain products. Our advice is always to bring a small first aid kit and try not to drink tap water .

ELECTRONIC DEVICES

PHOTOS and VIDEOS

There are no restrictions on cameras of any kind in Kenya. Our advice is to bring a good number of spare memory cards and batteries with you and use plastic cases or bags to protect your products from sand or dust.
We remind you that it is forbidden to photograph airports, military installations, government buildings, bridges and military personnel in uniform, furthermore it is always good practice to ask permission from people before photographing or ask the guide for advice.

TELEPHONY

The cell phone works almost everywhere but we advise you not to apply an extra “foreign” rate with your Italian operator which involves very high costs (often around €8/min). First of all, before taking off, deactivate ROAMING on your smartphone and upon arrival purchase a local rechargeable SIM (such as Safaricom or Airtel) which are easily available.
Upon your request we can bring it directly to you upon your arrival at the airport or hotel so that you can immediately notify your home of your arrival in Kenya. The costs are relatively low, for example with €10 you can have 3 or 5 GB of data traffic so you can use your cell phone with complete peace of mind during your stay. In this way, you can make calls through VOIP services such as Whatsapp, Skype or Messenger and not find strange surprises on your bill.

However, lodges, villages and many restaurants have free Wi-Fi that you can use for your connections.

POWER adapter

Although it is often easy to find them on site, we always remember to bring with you at least one Type G pin adapter (English type).
