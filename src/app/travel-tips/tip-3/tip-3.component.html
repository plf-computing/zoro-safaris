RULES DURING SAFARI.

A Few Rules for a 100% Safari

As in all National Parks, there are rules to follow that will allow you to fully enjoy nature and its inhabitants.

Read carefully and experience Kenya!


Always follow the instructions that our guide will give you, also respecting the agreed times.
Respect the privacy of wildlife, this is their habitat.
Be careful of animals, they are wild and can be unpredictable.
Do not approach animals or make sudden noises or movements.
Not feeding animals disrupts their diet and leads to human dependency.
Remain silent, noise disturbs wildlife and may disturb your fellow travellers.
Remain in your vehicle at all times except at picnic or designated walking areas.
When driving, keep below the maximum speed limit (40 km/h â€“ 25 mph).
Never drive off-road, seriously damaging the habitat.
When observing wildlife, keep a minimum distance of 20 metres and stay on the side of the road to allow others to pass.
Do not throw away waste but store it and throw it in the spaces outside the Parks.
Never leave fires unattended or throw burning objects.
Respect Kenya's cultural heritage, never photograph local people or their habitat without asking their permission.
Respect Kenyan cultural traditions and always dress modestly.
Stay or leave before sunset: Visitors must leave the park between 6:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. unless camping overnight.
Night driving is not permitted.
During the Safaris it is forbidden to bring flammable materials, alcohol, drugs and animals.
You will only be allowed to smoke in designated areas so we ask you not to do so during the Game Drives.
Inside the tented camps or lodges you are invited to comply with the structure's regulations which you can find displayed or by asking at reception.
Please inform your guide and lodges or tented camps of any allergies, disabilities or intolerances of any kind.
The use of drones is not permitted inside the Parks.
Do not lean out of the vehicle when stopped or while driving.
